What you're watching

40 triangles, each controlled by a neural network with 8 inputs and 8 hidden nodes. The inputs are wall proximity sensors and the direction and distance to the nearest food pellet. The outputs are turn rate and thrust.

No agent is told what food is, or that it should move toward it. That behavior has to emerge from selection pressure alone.

How it evolves
Score

Food eaten × 200. Time alive is a tie-breaker.

Select

Top 30% become parents. Bottom 70% are replaced each generation.

Crossover

Each child's 90 weights are drawn randomly from two parents.

Mutate

12% of weights get a random nudge. Top 2 carry over unchanged.

What to watch for

Gen 1–5: Random wandering. Food is eaten by accident.

Gen 10–20: The blue leader starts orienting toward food. Others follow.

Gen 30+: Efficient navigation. Watch the trails curve toward pellets.

Colors

Blue — current leader (highest score this generation).
Green — top quarter.
Gray — middle.
Dim — bottom quarter.
